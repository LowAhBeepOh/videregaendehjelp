Oppgavetyper og tidsbruk

Oppgavetyper

Eksamen i engelsk består av fire oppgavetyper: lytting og lesing, videreformidling av innhold, samhandling og skriftlig produksjon. Dette gir deg mulighet til å vise din kompetanse i så
stor del av faget som mulig ut fra eksamensformen. Hele læreplanen blir lagt til grunn for utforming av eksamen.

Tidsbruk

Siden eksamen varer i fem timer, anbefaler vi at du fordeler tiden din omtrent slik:
. Del 1 lytting og lesing - en time
. Del 2 videreformidling - en time
. Del 3 samhandling - en time
· Del 4 skriftlig produksjon - to timer (svar kun på én av oppgavene i del 4: A, B, C eller D)

History of Renewable Energy

Read the paragraphs below. Click and drag to put the paragraphs in the correct order. The first paragraph is given.

First paragraph: Renewable energy sources such as wind and solar power have become increasingly important in the global effort to combat climate change. Today, renewable energy accounts for about 30 percent of the world's electricity generation, up from just 5 percent two decades ago.

The first windmills were built in Persia around 900 AD, primarily used for pumping water and grinding grain. Modern wind turbines, however, were not developed until the 20th century when electricity became a global necessity. The Danish engineer Charles F. Brush built the first electricity-generating wind turbine in 1888.

Solar energy technology dates back centuries, with ancient civilizations using passive solar design in their buildings. However, the first modern solar panel was invented in 1954 by scientists at Bell Labs in New Jersey. This breakthrough led to the solar cells used in satellites and later, residential solar systems.

The 1973 oil crisis marked a turning point in renewable energy adoption. Oil-producing nations cut off supplies, causing severe shortages and economic turmoil. This crisis forced governments and industries to seriously consider alternatives to fossil fuels, leading to massive investments in renewable energy research.

In the 21st century, technological advances have made renewable energy more affordable and efficient. Solar panel costs have dropped by 90 percent since 2010, while wind turbines have become more powerful. Many countries now aim to transition to 100 percent renewable energy by 2050.

Interestingly, renewable energy is not a new concept. Many ancient cultures relied on wind and water power for essential tasks. The transition back to renewable energy represents a return to traditional practices combined with modern innovation.
